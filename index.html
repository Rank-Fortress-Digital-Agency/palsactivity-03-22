NanniesNannies

There are different types of child care. Child care can also be undertaken by a nanny. Nannies can take care of your child in your home, or they can watch your child in their own home. There are pros and cons to having a nanny.


Pros:

1. You will have control

If you hire nannies, you can give them a list of what they should do and not do. You will be able to tell them how you want your child to study.

2. You choose what they eat

Since they are more likely to stay at your home, you can also control your child’s diet. You can suggest what your child should eat for breakfast, lunch, and snacks.

3. Personalized experience

This service will be one on one. Therefore, your child will be given a hundred percent of the attention. We can ensure that the nanny will be more focused and the service will be more personalized.


Cons:

1. They can be expensive

Compared to other child care options for working parents, hiring nannies can be expensive. As they will only focus on your child, they will, of course, charge you a little higher.

2. Providing the tools

Yes, you will be obliged to provide a room for tutoring. You will also give the nanny tools for their service. So you might also consider buying some equipment first before hiring a nanny. Better yet, inquire first.

3. They may need their transportation

Some nannies will also charge you for their transportation expenses. If you follow a strict family budget, you should also consider this.

4. Lack of social life

Socializing with others has a significant impact on children’s growth. The more they associate with other children, the more they know how to act in public. The skills they will learn outside will take an essential part of their lives while growing up.

Ask Family and Friends for Help

If you’re still not comfortable leaving your little one with strangers, relying on family and friends is a good option for you. This is especially advantageous if you are on a low budget since your friends and family are more likely to be sympathetic. One disadvantage of this choice is that they have their own lives and may not always be accessible to assist you.

If you choose to have your relative take care of your child, do not expect much when it comes to development. They may be there for your child for physical support, but they can’t teach them the lessons you might get when you enroll them in a child care center.

No matter which option you choose, be sure to do your research and select a child care option that’s right for your family. You can rest assured knowing that your children are in good hands with the proper child care.
